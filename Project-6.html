Smart Health Monitoring & Alert System (SHMAS)

System to monitor health data and send real-time emergency alerts

Project Brief

A Smart Health Monitoring & Alert System (SHMAS) is an advanced healthcare solution designed to continuously track vital health parameters of individuals and provide real-time alerts in case of abnormalities or emergencies. This system is particularly useful for elderly people, patients with chronic diseases, and individuals requiring constant health supervision. By leveraging smart devices, sensors, and intelligent algorithms, SHMAS enhances patient safety, enables early detection of health issues, and facilitates timely medical intervention. The primary goal of SHMAS is to empower patients and healthcare providers with continuous health data, enabling proactive care rather than reactive treatment. It is designed to function both in-home and remotely, making healthcare accessible and efficient without constant physical visits to clinics or hospitals. Core Features and Functionalities: Real-Time Health Monitoring: SHMAS integrates with wearable devices and medical sensors to continuously collect data such as heart rate, blood pressure, blood oxygen levels (SpO2), body temperature, glucose levels, and ECG signals. These sensors transmit data in real-time to a centralized platform. Data Processing and Analysis: The system processes raw sensor data using embedded algorithms or cloud-based analytics to detect abnormalities or trends indicating potential health risks, such as irregular heartbeats, hypertension, hypoxia, or hyperglycemia. Alert and Notification System: When abnormal readings are detected, SHMAS immediately sends alerts to the patient, caregivers, and healthcare providers through multiple channels like SMS, email, or mobile app notifications. This timely alert can prevent medical emergencies or provide faster response times. Historical Data and Trends: SHMAS stores health data over time, enabling visualization of trends and patterns through graphs and reports. This historical perspective helps doctors make informed decisions about treatment plans and adjustments. Remote Access and Telemedicine Integration: Healthcare providers can remotely monitor patients' health status via dashboards, enabling teleconsultations and reducing the need for in-person visits. This is particularly important during situations like pandemics or for patients in remote areas.
